Why: Oil lubricates and protects the engine. Over time: it becomes dirty loses viscosity stops protecting engine parts properly
Symptoms: Engine becomes louder, Ticking/knocking noises, Burning smell, Very dark thin oil, Oil warning light
Risks if Ignored: Engine wear, Engine seizure, Turbo damage
Parts to Replace: Engine oil, Oil filter

# Spark Plugs
When to Change: Regular plugs: every 20,000–30,000 km, Iridium/platinum: every 60,000–100,000 km
Why: Spark plugs ignite the air/fuel mixture. Over time they wear out and produce weaker sparks.
Symptoms: Rough idle, Misfires, Hard starting, Poor fuel economy, Loss of power
Risks if Ignored: Catalytic converter damage, Poor engine performance
Parts to Replace: Spark plugs
Also Consider: Sometimes ignition coils too

# Car Battery
When to Change: Every 3–5 years
Why: Battery loses charging capacity with age.
Symptoms: Slow engine crank, Electrical problems, Dim headlights
Risks if Ignored: Car won’t start
Parts to Replace: Battery
[SEP]
# Transmission Fluid
When to Change: Automatic: 40,000–80,000 km, Manual: varies
Why: Lubricates gears and transmission components.
Symptoms: Hard shifting, Slipping gears, Delayed acceleration
Risks if Ignored: Transmission failure
Parts to Replace: Transmission fluid
Also Consider: Sometimes transmission filter
[SEP]
# Tires
When to Change: Usually 40,000–70,000 km, Or when tread is worn
Symptoms: Cracks, Uneven wear, Poor grip, Vibrations
Risks if Ignored: Blowout, Poor braking, Loss of control
Parts to Replace: Tires
Also Consider: Wheel alignment sometimes needed
[SEP]
# Cabin Air Filter
When to Change: Every 15,000–25,000 km, Or once a year
Why: Filters dust, pollen, and pollution from the air entering the cabin.
Symptoms: Weak AC airflow, Bad smell inside car, Foggy windows, More dust inside cabin
Risks if Ignored: Poor air quality, AC system strain
Parts to Replace: Cabin air filter
[SEP]
# Water Pump
When to Change: Often around 80,000–150,000 km, Usually replaced with timing belt
Why: Circulates coolant through the engine.
Symptoms: Coolant leak, Engine overheating, Whining noise, Steam from engine bay
Risks if Ignored: Severe overheating, Engine damage
Parts to Replace: Water pump, Coolant
[SEP]
# Thermostat
When to Change: Usually when faulty
Why: Controls coolant flow and engine temperature.
Symptoms: Engine overheating, Temperature fluctuates, Heater not working properly
Risks if Ignored: Overheating, Poor fuel economy
Parts to Replace: Thermostat
Also Consider: Coolant sometimes
[SEP]
# Serpentine Belt / Accessory Belt
When to Change: Every 60,000–100,000 km
Why: Drives alternator, AC compressor, power steering pump, etc.
Symptoms: Squealing noise, Cracks on belt, Weak battery charging, Loss of power steering
Risks if Ignored: Car overheating, Battery not charging, Steering becomes hard
Parts to Replace: Serpentine belt
Also Consider: Belt tensioner sometimes
[SEP]
# Brake Fluid
When to Change: Every 2 years
Why: Brake fluid absorbs moisture over time.
Symptoms: Soft brake pedal, Reduced braking performance, Dark brake fluid
Risks if Ignored: Brake failure
Parts to Replace: Brake fluid
[SEP]
# Brake Rotors / Discs
When to Change: Usually 50,000–100,000 km, Depends on driving style
Symptoms: Steering wheel vibration while braking, Grinding noise, Grooves on rotors
Risks if Ignored: Weak braking, Damaged brake system
Parts to Replace: Fuel injectors, Injector seals
[SEP]
# Turbocharger
When to Change: Usually 150,000–250,000 km, Depends heavily on oil maintenance
Symptoms: Whining sound, Blue smoke, Loss of power, Oil consumption
Risks if Ignored: Engine damage, Turbo failure
Parts to Replace: Turbocharger
Also Consider: Oil lines sometimes
[SEP]
# Suspension Shock Absorbers
When to Change: Around 80,000–120,000 km
Why: Absorb road impacts and stabilize vehicle.
Symptoms: Bouncy ride, Oil leaking from shocks, Uneven tire wear, Poor handling
Risks if Ignored: Loss of control, Longer braking distance
Parts to Replace: Shock absorbers/struts
[SEP]
# Ball Joints
When to Change: When worn
Symptoms: Clunking noise, Steering vibration, Uneven tire wear
Risks if Ignored: Suspension failure, Loss of steering control
Parts to Replace: Ball joints
[SEP]
# Wheel Bearings
When to Change: Usually 100,000–150,000 km
Symptoms: Humming noise, Grinding noise, Vibration increasing with speed
Risks if Ignored: Wheel instability, Wheel locking
Parts to Replace: Wheel bearing
Also Consider: Wheel hub sometimes
[SEP]
# Alternator
When to Change: Usually 120,000–200,000 km
Symptoms: Battery warning light, Flickering lights, Dead battery repeatedly
Risks if Ignored: Vehicle shuts down while driving
Parts to Replace: Alternator
Also Consider: Serpentine belt sometimes
[SEP]
# AC Compressor
When to Change: When faulty
Symptoms: No cold air, Clicking noises, AC clutch not engaging
Risks if Ignored: AC system contamination
Parts to Replace: AC compressor, Refrigerant recharge
[SEP]
# Wheel Alignment
When to Change: After hitting potholes, After suspension work, Every 20,000 km check
Symptoms: Car pulls left/right, Uneven tire wear, Crooked steering wheel
Risks if Ignored: Tire wear, Poor handling
